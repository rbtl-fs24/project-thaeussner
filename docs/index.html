Methods

The data was obtained using a Google Forms survey. Over the span of nine days from 2024-05-21 to 2024-05-29, students were asked on campus or online to fill out the questionnaire. The answers were automatically gathered in Google Sheets and from there converted to csv and rds using /R/01-data_download.R .

Respondents

In the first section, respondents were asked to state their age, study field and level, and their institution. In Figure 1 (a), we can see that most answers came from people within Engineering at ETH, and that all respondents were in their 20s as seen in Figure 1 (b).

In the next questions, the respondents were asked to rank 1) the general importance of waste reduction and 2) the general importance of recycling on a scale of 1 (not important at all) to 5 (very important). The answers are summarized in Figure 3, where it can be seen that both are perceived as important by most, with waste reduction having a higher mean importance than recycling.
